With your knee history and current recovery limitations, your edge will not come from intensity. It will come from restraint, structure, and repetition.

Zone 2 builds your aerobic engine without unnecessary joint stress. Threshold sessions improve your ability to sustain faster efforts without drifting into uncontrolled fatigue. Strength training supports the tissues that allow you to keep running week after week.

Training notes and definitions

Zone 2 running is low-intensity aerobic work performed at a pace where you can comfortably hold a conversation. It strengthens your aerobic base and supports long-term endurance development.

Threshold training represents controlled, sustainable discomfort. It is not sprinting and it is not maximal effort. Breathing should be elevated but stable. The goal is rhythm, not aggression.

Controlled tempo sits slightly below threshold intensity and prioritizes posture, breathing control, and pacing discipline.

Progressive overload means that training demand increases gradually over time. Adaptation occurs when stress is applied consistently, not suddenly. Sudden spikes are avoided deliberately to protect long-term progression.
